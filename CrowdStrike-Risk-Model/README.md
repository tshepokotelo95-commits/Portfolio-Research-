## Cybersecurity Valuation Floor & Outage Stress-Test Model

### CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: CRWD) — Event-Driven Risk Analysis

---

### Project Overview
This project features an institutional-grade **Event-Driven Financial Stress-Test and Scenario Model** engineered in the wake of the July 2024 global IT outage. Moving beyond standard steady-state assumptions, this model was built to distinguish short-term, headline-driven market panic from permanent enterprise value destruction. By stress-testing catastrophic risk variables, the model isolates CrowdStrike’s structural **"valuation floor"** to evaluate the asset through a downside-protection growth equity framework.

### Core Modeling Pillars & Stress Variables
The engine focuses on three highly sensitive operational disruption vectors over a 3-to-5-year forecasting horizon:
* **Customer Churn & Logo Retention Triage:** Quantifying the fiscal impact of contract non-renewals, delayed sales cycles, and competitive migrations to alternatives like Palo Alto Networks and SentinelOne.
* **Pricing Power & Margin Contraction:** Modeling annualized recurring revenue (ARR) impacts driven by aggressive discounting, expanded service-level agreement (SLA) credit pay-outs, and defensive retention incentives.
* **Legal and Insurance Liability Capping:** Factoring in deferred revenue adjustments, legal fee outlays, and insurance backstop limitations to stress-test free cash flow (FCF) resilience.

### Key Analytical Takeaway
The model concludes that while near-term cash flows absorb heavy operational friction from SLA penalties and legal contingencies, the architectural stickiness of the Falcon platform preserves long-term enterprise value. The severe downside scenario establishes a fundamental valuation floor at **$185.00**, representing an asymmetric entry point where the asset becomes an undeniable target for a technology take-private or institutional growth buyout.
